When CampusXATL hit a GTM stall with our initial go-to-market strategy, I recognized the need for a strategic pivot. Instead of continuing down an ineffective path, I completely redesigned our approach to user acquisition. By shifting from high-cost traditional lead sources to a scrappy, direct-outreach prospecting model, we not only bypassed expensive channels but also established a more authentic connection with our target users. This pivot resulted in securing 100 daily sign-ups and laid the foundation for a scalable, low-CAC pipeline that transformed our growth trajectory.

During the Georgia Tech AI Hackathon, we encountered a critical technical block with our ML model that threatened to derail our entire MVP. With only hours remaining in the 72-hour sprint, I made the decisive call to pivot from our complex machine learning approach to a rule-based logic system. This strategic shift allowed us to maintain the core functionality of our biometric hardware MVP while ensuring we could deliver a working prototype. The pivot not only saved our project but also demonstrated my ability to make quick, pragmatic decisions under pressure. We successfully decoded live heart-rate signals and delivered actionable analytics for Fortune 500 entertainment clients, proving that sometimes the best solution is the one that works.
